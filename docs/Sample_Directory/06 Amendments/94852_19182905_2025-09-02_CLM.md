CLAIM OR CLAIMS

1. (currently amended) A method for improving corneal health prior to cataract surgery in a patient; the method comprising:
    1. identifying a patient in need of cataract surgery,
    2. diagnosing an ocular surface condition in the patient that requires therapeutic treatment,
    3. preparing an amniotic membrane by verifying that it is sterile and properly sized for the patient’s eye,
    4. applying said amniotic membrane to said eye without performing any corneal debridement to improve corneal health prior to obtaining biometric measurements for intraocular lens selection, thereby ensuring enhanced accuracy in intraocular lens selection which improves a visual ability of the subject following placement,
    5. allowing regenerative and anti-inflammatory factors of said amniotic membrane to be absorbed by said eye, and
    6. evaluating the ocular surface health of the patient after said applying step; wherein
    7. said amniotic membrane is dehydrated, and
    8. said amniotic membrane is applied to the ocular surface without requiring orientation of an epithelial side relative to the cornea.
2. (currently amended) The method for improving corneal health prior to cataract surgery in a patient of claim 1, wherein applying said amniotic membrane to said eye comprises:
    1. preparing said amniotic membrane by ensuring its sterility and sizing it for application, and
    2. placing said amniotic membrane onto the ocular surface beneath a contact lens to secure it in position.
3. (currently amended) The method for improving corneal health prior to cataract surgery in a patient of claim 2, further comprising:
    1. smoothing said amniotic membrane with a surgical sponge during application to ensure even coverage and adherence.
4. (currently amended) The method for improving corneal health prior to cataract surgery in a patient of claim 2, further comprising:
    1. monitoring for initial discomfort after said applying step and ensuring that said amniotic membrane remains correctly positioned on the eye.
5. (currently amended) The method for improving corneal health prior to cataract surgery in a patient of claim 1, further comprising:
    1. monitoring the healing of the corneal epithelium after applying said amniotic membrane, and
    2. conducting a corneal assessment to evaluate healing and ocular surface improvement.
6. (currently amended) The method for improving corneal health prior to cataract surgery in a patient of claim 5, further comprising:
    1. repeating the application of said amniotic membrane if said corneal assessment indicates that the corneal epithelium has not fully healed.
7. (currently amended) The method for improving corneal health prior to cataract surgery in a patient of claim 1, further comprising:
    1. performing biometric measurements on the patient’s eye after the corneal health is improved,
    2. selecting an intraocular lens for the cataract surgery based on the improved ocular surface condition.
8. (currently amended) The method for improving corneal health prior to cataract surgery in a patient of claim 1, further comprising:
    1. maintaining said amniotic membrane is maintained on the eye for at least one week as an extended application to address a corneal trauma, and
    2. monitoring the patient’s recovery is monitored prior to performing the biometric measurements.
