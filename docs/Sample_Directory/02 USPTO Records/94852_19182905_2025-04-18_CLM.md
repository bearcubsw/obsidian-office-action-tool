CLAIM OR CLAIMS

1. A method for preoperative preparation to facilitate the therapeutic application of an amniotic membrane to an eye, leveraging its regenerative and anti-inflammatory properties to enhance corneal health prior to surgery and thereby improve visual outcome postoperatively; comprising:
    1. identifying patients requiring cataract surgery,
    2. diagnosing ocular surface conditions,
    3. applying said amniotic membrane to said eye to improve corneal health prior to biometric measurements, thereby ensuring enhanced accuracy in intraocular lens selection which improves visual outcome postoperatively,
    4. allowing the therapeutic properties of said amniotic membrane to be absorbed by said eye, and
    5. evaluating the patient’s ocular surface health after applying said amniotic membrane.
2. The method for preoperative preparation of claim 1, wherein applying said amniotic membrane to said eye comprises:
    1. preparing said amniotic membrane by ensuring its sterility and sizing it for application, and
    2. placing said amniotic membrane onto the ocular surface beneath a contact lens to secure it in position.
3. The method for preoperative preparation of claim 2, further comprising:
    1. smoothing said amniotic membrane with a surgical sponge during application to ensure even coverage and adherence.
4. The method for preoperative preparation of claim 2, further comprising:
    1. monitoring the patient for initial discomfort and ensuring correct placement of said contact lens.
5. The method for preoperative preparation of claim 1, further comprising:
    1. monitoring patient recovery after applying said amniotic membrane, and
    2. conducting a corneal assessment to evaluate healing and ocular surface improvement.
6. The method for preoperative preparation of claim 5, further comprising:
    1. repeating the application of said amniotic membrane if said corneal assessment indicates incomplete healing.
7. The method for preoperative preparation of claim 1, further comprising:
    1. conducting biometric measurements to assess ocular parameters,
    2. selecting an intraocular lens based on the optimized ocular surface, and
    3. finalizing a surgical plan for cataract surgery.
8. The method for preoperative preparation of claim 1, further comprising:
    1. incorporating extended applications of said amniotic membrane to address significant corneal trauma, and
    2. monitoring recovery before conducting biometric measurements.
9. A method of ocular surface optimization using an amniotic membrane in combination with a contact lens, the method comprising:
    1. preparing said amniotic membrane by ensuring its sterility,
    2. placing said amniotic membrane inside said contact lens prior to application on an eye,
    3. positioning said contact lens and said amniotic membrane onto said eye such that said amniotic membrane contacts the cornea,
    4. maintaining said contact lens in place for a time sufficient to allow regenerative and anti-inflammatory factors to absorb into said eye, and
    5. removing said contact lens or said amniotic membrane after verifying an improvement in corneal health or reduction in dry eye symptoms.
10. The method of claim 9, further comprising:
    1. smoothing said amniotic membrane with a surgical sponge prior to placing said contact lens and said amniotic membrane onto said eye, ensuring even contact with the corneal surface.
11. The method of claim 9, further comprising:
    1. capturing microscopic or photographic documentation to confirm that said amniotic membrane remains centered on said eye during the duration of said application.
12. The method of claim 9, wherein:
    1. said amniotic membrane comprises a cryopreserved or dehydrated membrane having regenerative and anti-inflammatory properties effective for either preoperative preparation or chronic dry eye relief.
13. The method of claim 9, further comprising:
    1. using said amniotic membrane for both preoperative ocular surface optimization and standalone dry eye treatment, wherein the same placement procedure is adaptable based on the patient’s surgical or non-surgical needs.
